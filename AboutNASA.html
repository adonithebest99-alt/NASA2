History Goals Missions Visions

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) is the United States’ leading organization for space exploration and scientific discovery. Since its founding in 1958, NASA has pushed the boundaries of human knowledge, exploring Earth, the Moon, Mars, and beyond.

Main Historical Events of NASA

  1. Foundation of NASA(1958)

    ●NASA was officially created by the U.S government under President Dwight D. Eisenhower in 1958
    ●This marked the beginning of the U.S space program

  2. Apollo 11 Moon Landing(1969)

    Apollo 11 Moon Landing made history when Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon, proving space travel beyond Earth was possible.

  3. Space Shutter Era(1981-2011)

    The Space Shuttle program introduced reusable spacecraft like Columbia and Discovery. It helped launch satellites, conduct experiments, and build the International Space Station.

  4. Curiosity Rover on Mars(2012)

    The Curiosity Rover landed on Mars to study its surface and search for signs of past life, sending valuable data about the planet’s history.

  5. International Space Station Begins(1998)

    NASA and international partners began building the ISS, starting with the Zarya module. It became a long-term home for astronauts in space and a major science laboratory in orbit.

Goals

  1. Explore space beyond Earth (Moon, Mars, deep space)
  2. Advance scientific knowledge in astronomy, physics, and Earth science
  3. Develop advanced space technology and spacecraft systems
  4. Study and monitor Earth’s climate and environment
  5. Support human space exploration and long-term space habitation

Visions

  1. To explore the unknown in space for the benefit of humanity
  2. To improve life on Earth through space-based discoveries
  3. To enable humans to live and work beyond Earth
  4. To expand human presence to the Moon, Mars, and beyond
  5. To lead global space exploration through innovation and collaboration
